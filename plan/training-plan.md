**Athlete:** Kyle | **Age:** 39 | **Erg:** Concept2 RowErg / PM5  
**Current 2k:** 7:35.7 (Mar 8, 2026) | **Target 2k:** 6:55.0 | **Gap:** 40.7s

**Schedule:** 3 sessions/week

## Context

Kyle rowed the Pete Plan daily through COVID, hitting a 6:55 2k in Dec 2020. Since then, rowing has been warmup/cooldown only (~1,300m avg session). The deconditioning gap is fitness and specificity, not athletic ceiling.

**Key finding:** All three Week 1 sessions showed the same pattern — pacing conservatively then finishing dramatically faster. The nervous system is sandbagging. Training the throttle is as important as building the engine.

**Updated Week 1 C zone:** 1:35–1:38 (was 1:40–1:44). Short-interval capacity is well ahead of 2k test pace.

## Session Architecture

**Session A — Aerobic Infrastructure**  
Long, controlled, UT1–UT2. Format rotates weekly. The compounding interest session.

**Session B — The Spice Session**  
Different interval format every week. 30/30s, Norwegians, broken 2ks, rate ladders, pyramids. Never the same twice in a cycle.

**Session C — Speed & Race Specificity**  
Short, sharp, high intent. Teaches the body what fast feels like. Increasingly race-specific through Phase 3.

---

## Phase 1: Rebuild the Engine (Weeks 1–4)
*Goal: Aerobic base, fix pacing discipline, stop protecting yourself*

### Week 1 ✅
- **A:** 30' steady state @ 2:05–2:10, rate 20 — *Result: 2:07.8 avg, on target*
- **B:** Pyramid v1:00/1:00r ×7 @ 1:52–1:55 — *Result: 1:51.0 avg, neg split to 1:43.4 on final 1'*
- **C:** 8×250m @ **1:35–1:38** (revised), 2' rest — *Result: 1:36.9 avg, neg split 1:38→1:35*

### Week 2
- **A:** 3×12' @ 2:02–2:05, 3' rest. Track avg pace per piece — look for consistency.
- **B:** 30/30s — 30" at 1:48, 30" easy × 16 rounds. Rate 26 on hard strokes. First time you'll feel this format.
- **C:** 4×500m @ 1:50–1:52, 3' rest. Use pace boat. Target variance < 1s across all 4.

### Week 3
- **A:** 40' steady state @ 2:08, rate 18. Low rate, long strokes. Volume is the mechanism.
- **B:** "Broken 4k" — 4×1k with 45" pause, target 1:55 splits. One continuous mental effort.
- **C:** 6×1' @ 1:45–1:48, 2' rest. Race-adjacent pace. Get familiar with the feeling.

### Week 4 — Benchmark
- **A:** 20' easy paddle
- **B:** 4×4' @ best sustainable pace, **3' rest** (not 4'). Record splits. True baseline.
- **C:** 4k time trial @ ~1:55. Honest pacing, not all-out.

---

## Phase 2: Raise the Floor (Weeks 5–8)
*Goal: Make AT feel like UT1. This is where 1:52 stops being a ceiling.*

### Week 5
- **A:** 2×20' @ 2:00–2:03, 4' rest
- **B:** Rate ladder — 4×4' at fixed pace 1:54, rate goes 22/24/26/28 each piece
- **C:** 8×500m alternating: odd @ 1:52, even @ 1:46. Full recovery.

### Week 6
- **A:** 45' steady state @ 2:05, rate 20
- **B:** Norwegian 10×2' @ 1:48–1:50, 2' rest
- **C:** 3×2' @ 1:43–1:45, 4' rest. Race pace. Get familiar before you need to hold it.

### Week 7
- **A:** 3×15' @ 2:00, 3' rest. Negative split each piece (start 2:03, finish 1:57)
- **B:** "Descending hell" — 6'/5'/4'/3'/2'/1' @ 1:52, rest = half the piece
- **C:** 6×1k @ 1:50–1:52, 3' rest

### Week 8 — Retest
- **A:** 20' easy
- **B:** 4×4' @ 3' rest — target 1:49–1:51 if Phase 2 working
- **C:** **2k all-out attempt.** Open 1:52–1:53 and hold. Target: 7:10–7:20.

---

## Phase 3: Race Specificity (Weeks 9–12)
*Goal: 1:43.75 becomes familiar, not foreign*

### Week 9
- **A:** 2×20' @ 1:58–2:00, 4' rest
- **B:** "Race simulation halves" — 4×(500m @ 1:43 + 500m easy), 4' between sets
- **C:** 10×250m @ 1:38–1:40, 90" rest. Faster than race pace — teaches headroom.

### Week 10
- **A:** 40' steady state @ 2:05
- **B:** 3×(4×1') — 1' hard @ 1:43, 1' easy × 4, 4' between sets. 12 race-pace minutes.
- **C:** 2×1k @ 1:45–1:47, 5' rest

### Week 11 — Sharpen
- **A:** 20' easy, technique focus only
- **B:** 4×500m @ 1:43, 5' rest. Should feel controlled, not desperate.
- **C:** 4k @ 1:50. Legs-check before the PR attempt.

### Week 12 — Peak
- **A:** 15' easy. Trust the work.
- **B:** 4×(2×1') @ race pace, very full recovery. Wake the legs, don't drain them.
- **C:** **2k PR attempt. Target: 6:55.**

---

## The Rules

1. **Easy days actually easy.** UT2 at 2:12+ feels embarrassingly slow. Do it anyway.
2. **Race pace is a separate skill.** Your body needs reps at 1:43 before it can race at 1:43 for 7 minutes.
3. **Tighten rest to 3 minutes** on 4×4' sessions. The classic Norwegian protocol uses 3', not 4'.
4. **Rate is a variable, not a constant.** Most intervals at rate 24–26. Race at 30–32. Don't train at 28 every session.
5. **Use the pace boat.** Set it to target pace from stroke one. Stop sandbagging the first half.
